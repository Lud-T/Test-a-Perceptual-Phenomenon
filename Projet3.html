    Question 2a: Establish hypotheses¶

    Null and alternative hypotheses are clearly stated in words and mathematically. Symbols in the mathematical statement are defined.

    comments from reviewer

    The Null and alternative hypotheses are not accurate, it is not clear if the hypotheses concerned about the population or the sample. It is stated "No difference in terms of time to read the text between incongruent and congruent text type", but instead the hypothesis should be concerned with the mean population time. The idea of the test is that we are using limited data (based on our samples) in order to make inferences about the populations (and the population means). We know what the sample means are, and we are trying to infer something about the population, so the null and alternative hypotheses should be concerned with the population. The link here includes an example for the null and alternative hypotheses. https://courses.lumenlearning.com/introstats1/chapter/null-and-alternative-hypotheses/ Please include also the mathematical statement of the hypothesis.

    • µC : mean time to read from the for Congruent words µI : mean time to read for Incongruent words

    Null hypothesis : H0
    No difference between the mean time to read of the two groups.

    H0 : µC = µI

    Alternative hypothesis : Ha
    There is a significative difference between the mean time to read of the two groups.

    H0 : µC != µI
